# 👣 Step-by-step guides

A **detailed guide per lab**: prerequisites, exact commands, *"what you'll see"* (real output),
*"what happened?"*, checkpoints, troubleshooting, bonus challenge, and **how to teach it to your students**.

> They complement the quick references in each folder (`02-docker/comandos-docker.en.md`, etc.).
> Here you go **hand-in-hand, step by step**; there you get the command cheat sheet.

## 🪟🐧 On Windows?

- **[PowerShell cheat sheet](windows-powershell.en.md)** — translation of the Unix commands (`curl`→`curl.exe`, etc.).
- **[Real Linux terminal with WSL2](terminal-linux-wsl.en.md)** — Ubuntu or Arch; run the guide commands **as-is**, no translation.

## 🧑‍🏫 How to use them

- **As an instructor:** project them and go step by step; each one includes *"what to demo"* and *"how to rescue"*.
- **As a participant:** follow at your own pace; each step tells you exactly what to type and what you should see.
- **As a lab for your students:** every guide closes with *"How to teach it to your students"* — classroom-ready ideas.

> Just starting? Begin with the [Lab 0 guide](00-ambiente.en.md) and follow the order. Each guide
> links to the next at the end. 🚀
